## Signs Your Chimney May Need Repair

- White staining (efflorescence) running down the brick face
- Mortar joints that look recessed, cracked, or missing between bricks
- Chunks of concrete or mortar appearing on the roof or in the gutters
- Cracks visible in the chimney crown or flashing area
- Water stains on the ceiling near the fireplace
- Bricks that are spalling — popping off in layers or flaking badly
- A chimney that hasn't been assessed in 10+ years

## Why Waiting Costs More

The chimney gets more UV, wind, rain, and freeze-thaw cycling than any other masonry surface on the house. That concentrated exposure means problems develop faster than on a protected wall. A crown that's cracked but hasn't opened up yet will open up. Mortar that's recessed but still present will eventually fail completely.

Once water is moving into the chimney structure, it can find its way into framing, insulation, and interior finishes. Repairing that damage costs far more than the original masonry repair would have. We've seen jobs that could have been a day's work turn into multi-week projects because water damage was allowed to develop.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should a chimney be inspected for masonry damage?**
A visual check every few years is a reasonable habit for any masonry chimney. If you've never had it assessed and you've owned the home for more than a decade, it's time. We can tell you honestly whether anything needs attention.

**Is chimney repair something I can put off until spring?**
You can schedule spring work now, but if there are open cracks or failing mortar letting water in, winter isn't kind to those conditions. Freeze-thaw damage compounds through the season. If you suspect active water infiltration, earlier is better.

**My chimney looks fine from the ground. Is that a reliable assessment?**
Not really. Most chimney damage starts at the crown and upper courses, which aren't visible from ground level. A proper assessment requires getting a closer look — either from the roof or with binoculars at minimum.

**Can you match my brick if some need to be replaced?**
We carry a range of salvaged and new brick and do our best to get a close visual match. Exact matches on older brick aren't always possible, but we'll be transparent about how close we can get before any work starts.

**What's the difference between chimney tuckpointing and a chimney rebuild?**
Tuckpointing replaces the mortar joints while the brick remains in place. A rebuild means removing and resetting the brick — typically from the roofline up — when the brick itself is too damaged to repair in place. We'll tell you clearly which situation you're in.
